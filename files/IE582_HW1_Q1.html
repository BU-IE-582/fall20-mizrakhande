expectedvaluehome=c()
samplemeans=mean(home_goals)
for (j in 0:max(home_goals)){
    expectedvaluehome[j+1]=dpois(j, samplemeans)*length(home_goals)
  }
hist(home_goals,xlab="Home Goals",ylab = " Number of Games",ylim = c(0,300),right=FALSE)
par(new=TRUE)
plot(c(0.5,1.5,2.5,3.5,4.5,5.5,6.5,7.5,8.5), expectedvaluehome, type= "b",xaxt="n", yaxt="n", ann=FALSE,col="red",xlim=c(0,8))

The distributions of home and away goals look like Poisson distribution. To prove that mean of home_goals is calculated and it is used as of a Poisson distribution. By using dpois(#ofGoals, ) command the probability of getting that amount of goal is calculated. Subsequently, this probability is multiplied by number of observations (number of total games) to get expected value. Then, it is plotted to the home_goals histogram.
